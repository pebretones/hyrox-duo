So, what is Hyrox?

Hyrox is a fitness race anyone can do. There's no gymnastics, no Olympic lifting, no surprises. Just running, rowing, carrying, and pushing — the same format worldwide.

You run 1 km, complete one workout station, repeat eight times, then finish with 100 wall balls. That's it.

Why do it as a duo?

In doubles, you race with a partner. You run every kilometre together, but split the station work however you like — one works while the other rests.

It's half the work, twice the fun. Perfect if you're new to fitness racing, or if you just want to share the pain with someone you like enough.

The Doubles Format

Both partners run each 1 km together. At each station, reps/distances are split freely between you — one works while the other rests. Only one partner at a time may perform the movement. Transitions count towards your total time, so every handoff matters.

Core Principles for Duos

  • Communicate in short numbers, not sentences
  • Match heart-rate, not ego — pace to the weaker runner
  • Pre-agreed splits beat improvisation
  • Transitions are a discipline — practice them
  • Encourage before you suffer

Weekly Training Plan

A 12-week build for duos split into three phases. Train together at least twice a week — partner chemistry is a muscle too.

Pacing guidelines

  • Base: easy conversational runs, technique before intensity, learn the movements
  • Build: Race-specific intervals, simulate 2-3 station combos together
  • Peak: Full or half simulations, taper last 7 days, rehearse transitions
  • Rest days are non-negotiable — duos need synchronized recovery
